Summary

  • Card type detection happens post-presentation: Customers select "card" without specifying credit or debit. The system detects card type after presentation. Model A applies surcharge only to credit cards. Model B treats all cards identically at the listed price.
  • Model A uses lower listed prices with post-detection surcharge: Shelf price is $100. After card presentation, credit pays $103 (surcharge applied), debit pays $100 (no surcharge allowed by law). Pre-card disclosure: "Card payments may include up to 3% surcharge."
  • Model B offers two display modes: Mode 1 shows single price ($103.25) with discount at payment. Mode 2 shows dual prices equally on shelf (card: $103.25, cash: $100.15). Rounding to nearest $0.25 means cash price is slightly above $100 after 3% discount ($100.15 vs. perfect $100).
  • Model B math uses proper discount backing-out: To achieve ~$100 cash price after 3% discount, listed price is $100 ÷ 0.97 = $103.093, rounded to $103.25 for consumer clarity. When 3% discount applied: $103.25 × 0.97 = $100.15. This slight overage is acceptable for practical display purposes.
  • Tax treatment (all models): Tax is calculated on the pre-discount/list price because that is the advertised price. In Model B, tax is calculated on $103.25 (the list price) for both card and cash customers. Discounts apply to subtotal only, not the tax basis. Surcharges and convenience fees are added AFTER tax (non-taxable).
  • ACH convenience fee configuration: In Model C online and other convenience fee scenarios, merchants can choose whether ACH incurs the convenience fee or receives a discount/no fee. This is a merchant configuration choice, not mandated by the model. Some merchants apply convenience fees to all payment methods including ACH; others exclude ACH or offer ACH as the "no fee" option.
  • Model C is channel-based composition, not dynamic: POS uses Model A (surcharge on credit only) or dual pricing. Online/invoice uses convenience fee (applies to all payment types) or dual pricing. Each channel has ONE pre-configured model that does not change based on card type or runtime conditions.
