 Dual Pricing via ACH/Cash Discounting Platform Capability

Context 	
This initiative originated from a specific request from a new reseller, Dough (https://www.dough.cc), an affiliate of Moolah.
Dough’s core value proposition to merchants is true “zero fees” on all transactions, including Credit and Debit cards. ACH and cash will be treated as equivalent.

Critically, this requirement cannot be met through credit card surcharging because:
•	Surcharging cannot be applied to debit cards (illegal)
•	Surcharging is restricted or capped in several U.S. states
•	Surcharging introduces additional compliance and network rule complexity
The compliant way to deliver Dough’s promised value proposition is through cash discounting / dual pricing, where fees are embedded in the price and a discount/lower price is offered for cash equivalent payments (cash and ACH).

Rate Saver limitations
GoDaddy Payments’ existing fee-passing solution, Rate Saver, has validated demand for merchants who want to reduce or offset processing fees. However, learnings from anecdotal sales feedback and merchant interviews have surfaced key limitations:
•	Merchants expect “zero fees” per our advertising, but discover they still pay fees for processing debit. This creates confusion, objection handling, and perceived “bait and switch”
•	Merchants operating in surcharge restricted states (e.g., CA, NY, MA) are blocked 
•	Surcharge is capped by the processing fees.
These gaps became especially acute when assessing reseller led opportunities like Dough and other ISOs.
Why cash discounting / dual pricing?
Cash discounting / dual pricing is being pursued as a complementary fee program (not a replacement) because it uniquely provides:
•	True zero processing fees for merchants, including debit
•	Applicability across all 50 states
•	Coverage across all payment methods (credit, debit, ACH, cash)
•	Fewer network constraints and reduced longterm regulatory risk
•	A simpler merchant and sales narrative: fees are built into the card price
This model mirrors widely accepted realworld examples (e.g., gas stations) and aligns with Dough’s GTM promise while remaining compliant and scalable for GoDaddy Payments.
 

Cash Discounting vs Dual Pricing
Although often used interchangeably in conversation, cash discounting and dual pricing are two different presentations of the same underlying capabilities.

Cash Discounting
•	Listed / sticker price is the card (higher) price
•	A separate, explicit discount line item is shown when the customer pays with: 
o	Cash or ACH (treated as cash equivalent)
•	The discount must appear at checkout and on the receipt
•	Consumers initially see a single price, with the discount applied at checkout 
Example:
•	Item 1 price: $105
•	Item 2 price: $210
•	SubTotal: $315
•	5% Cash / ACH discount: –$15.75
•	Net paid (cash/ACH): $299.25

Dual Pricing
•	Two prices are always displayed on all consumer facing surfaces, including: 
o	Shelf price
o	Menu
o	Order/Invoice line items
o	Checkout screen
•	Every item has: 
o	A card price
o	A cash / ACH price
•	At checkout and on receipts: 
o	No separate discount line item is required
o	Only the selected price is shown

Example:
•	Item 1 
o	Card price: $105
o	Cash/ACH price: $99.75
•	Item 2 
o	Card price: $210
o	Cash/ACH price: $199.50
•	Card SubTotal: $315
•	Cash Sub Total: $299.25
Key rule: For a C2, no item has a single price: All items are  dual-listed everywhere the 
customer sees price of an item.
The rest of this document will focus on Dual Pricing as the product construct we will deliver. The above distinction is for clarity on contrasting presentation of the underlying capability. 
Legal & Compliance Constraints
The following constraints impact product & UX 
1.	Discounts must never be linked to processing costs
o	If the discount is framed or interpreted as “covering processing fees,” it will be treated as a surcharge, and surcharging debit is illegal. Therefore, discount logic must be decoupled from processing fees
2.	Downward discount only
o	Customers pay a lower price/receive a discount for preferred methods (cash/ACH), fees are never “added” at checkout, that’s an illegal surcharge.
3.	All states, no caps
o	Unlike surcharging, cash discounting / dual pricing: 
	Is legal in all 50 U.S. states
	Has no cap on the price difference
	Applies across all card types
4.	Mutual exclusivity with surcharge
o	Merchants cannot enable Rate Saver and dual pricing simultaneously
5.	Pricing visibility is mandatory
o	Wherever a customer sees a price, the experience must match the chosen model: 
	Cash discount → discount disclosed at checkout and receipt
	Dual pricing → both prices visible from first exposure
Violating any of the above creates legal and compliance risk for both merchants and GoDaddy.

Scoping and Phasing
This section defines what is included in the initial launch and what is intentionally deferred, to reduce blast radius while enabling Dough’s near-term needs and establishing a reusable/extensible platform capability. 
1.	Phase 1: MVP for Dough with Dual pricing on Invoicing (ACH preferred) and Mobile App (Cash preferred): 
Deliver core capabilities + invoicing and mobile experience to support true zero-fee narrative via dual pricing presentation.
Deliverables: Core engine (Standard Price /Preferred price for ACH/card), merchant opt-in including mutual exclusivity with credit surcharge, and attestation; C1 invoicing UI, C2 Invoicing presentation and receipts, Mobile/POS dual-price display + receipts with Cash as a preferred method, C1 post-order management including voids, refunds, chargebacks, order and transaction dashboards and reports, audit logging.
2.	Phase 2: Additional channels (POS, Pay links) and GoDaddy Direct: 
Extend the same engine and compliance model to in-person and paylinks
Deliverables: Support for GoDaddy Payments, packaging/positioning as part of Rate Saver (i.e. credit surcharge or dual pricing). C1 management with toggle on whether catalog price is standard or preferred.

Definition of Success  (Phase 1)
Success in phase 1 is enabling Dough’s Dual Pricing on invoicing and mobile, with real usage.
Metrics:
•	Dual pricing live on Invoicing & Mobile for Dough
•	Dough merchants issuing live dual-priced invoices and mobile sales
•	Sustained usage (MAS) of 30%+ of base.
•	Directional revenue: Credible path to ~$10M annualized GPV from Dough within 2 quarters of launch
Hard requirements:
•	Zero compliance incidents
•	No surcharge misclassification
•	Accurate price presentation on invoices, mobile, receipts, commerce home, dashboards and reports
Failure metrics:
•	Merchant or customer confusion
•	Pricing perceived as a surcharge
•	Operational issues or legal escalations

Acceptance Criteria: 
1.	Core calculation (Preferred price for ACH, Standard Price for Cards), 
a.	System must support:
•	Standard Price
•	Preferred Method Price 
o	For ACH on Invoicing (and future on Paylinks and eCommerce)
o	For Cash on Mobile (and future on POS)
b.	System must prevent any upward price add-on at checkout, or “Fee” terminology.
2.	Distributor Eligibility 
a.	Dual Pricing availability should be configurable at a distributor level. For Phase 1, this is only for Dough.
3.	Merchant Eligibility and opt-in
a.	Dough Merchants must be automatically enrolled into ACH at onboarding.
b.	Merchant must have ACH enabled to support dual pricing on Invoicing.
c.	Mutual exclusivity with credit surcharge
d.	Currently allowed in all US states, but might be restricted by jurisdiction in the future.
e.	Merchant must attest:
(i)	They understand pricing visibility obligations
1.	All merchandising pricing material (menus, boards, brochures, website) will reflect the dual pricing – i.e. ACH/cash and card pricing
2.	Place signs visible around their business stating their cash discount policy. For example, “Save 5% when you pay with cash.”
(ii)	They are independently setting prices/discounts and not linked to processing fees.
(iii)	Attestation must be timestamped and logged.
4.	Configuration at merchant level
a.	Enable Preferred Method Savings - on/off
b.	Discount % for preferred pricing
i.	ACH __%
ii.	Cash __% 
c.	Indicate whether their catalog/entered prices already include discount – yes/no
•	 If Catalog/Invoice prices already include the discount, then
Standard Price = Catalog price ÷ (1 - discount%)
Preferred Payment Method price = Catalog Price
•	If Catalog/Invoice prices do not already include the discount, then
Standard Price = Catalog price
Preferred Payment Method Price = Catalog price x (1 - discount%)
d.	Toggle on whether c2-facing displays should be as discounts or dual prices.
5.	C1 Create Invoice  UI
a.	Indicate to C1 that dual pricing is active, and whether amounts being entered are pre- or post- discount
b.	Highlight dual pricing on Invoice previews.  
6.	C2 Invoicing presentation and receipts
a.	At all points prior to payment where pricing is displayed (e.g. email, PDF, invoice web page, system must:
i.	Display dual pricing: standard price and discounted ACH price for each line item
ii.	Display total amount for Standard and discounted ACH.
b.	Require c2 selection of payment method and confirmation of amount before proceeding
c.	Receipt Requirements
i.	Receipt must always display the selected payment method.
ii.	For a card payment – receipt must display the Standard Prices, with no discounts. 
iii.	For a preferred payment method, receipt must display prices with discounts applied at line item level. (per current behavior?). 
iv.	No references to processing costs, surcharges or card fees
d.	If merchant has chosen to display as a discount instead of Dual pricing, the invoice and receipt must display discounts rather than dual pricing.

8.	C1 post-order management 
Manage the invoice and orders post-transaction, including voids, refunds/partial refunds, chargebacks etc.
a.	Refunds
Partial refunds must be issued proportionally based on the original payment method, and reverse discount proportionally. 
b.	Voids
Void must reverse the exact authorized amount.
9.	Order and transaction dashboards and reports
a.	Transaction dashboards and reports must reflect amounts based on selected payment method.
b.	Transaction amounts must reconcile to order amounts 
c.	Order reports and order item reports must reflect item-level price accuracy, including discounts and/or discounted and undiscounted prices.Standard Pricing with discounts applied if a preferred payment method was used.
10.	Additional Use Cases
a.	Tips / Gratuity
Tip calculations must be based on the selected payment method price and reflect final authorized amount transparently.
b.	Taxes
Taxes must be calculated on the final transaction subtotals (post-discount for cash/ACH, Standard Price for others), consistent with jurisdictional tax rules.
c.	Promotions / Coupons
Savings must apply after other promotional discounts unless merchant explicitly configures stacking order; stacking order must be documented and consistent.
d.	Split Tender
System must calculate and apply savings only to the relevant portion of the transaction; e.g. for in-person cash+card split tender, discount applied only to cash portion. 
11.	Audit logging
a.	The system must log:
•	Feature activation timestamp
•	Attestation acceptance
•	Pricing configuration changes
•	Payment method used and resultant order-item level pricing.
