# Mock Pack Strategy

## Why These Mocks

This mock pack is designed to surface the strategic tensions and real-world tradeoffs inherent in the "zero fees" product strategy. Each mock tests a specific hypothesis or constraint that could make or break merchant adoption.

---

## Mock 01 — Customer Price Comparison

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether merchants understand the fundamental difference between:
- Lower posted prices + visible fee (Model A)
- Higher posted prices + no surprise fee (Model B)
- Channel-based combinations (Model C)

### Tension Surfaced
**Posted price vs final price transparency**

Merchants must choose between:
- Attracting customers with lower shelf/menu prices but risking fee pushback
- Posting higher prices upfront but avoiding surprise fees

### Model Focus
All three models (A, B, C)

### Survey Alignment
- Section C: Meaning expectation
- Section D: Constraint tolerance
- Section H: Model comparison

### Why This Mock First
Establishes the core pricing reality before diving into mechanics. Forces merchants to confront the tradeoff before seeing implementation details.

## Mock 02 — Pricing Transparency

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether merchants believe their customers will perceive each model as transparent and compliant.

### Tension Surfaced
**What customers see vs what customers pay**

This mock explicitly shows:
- Shelf/menu/site presentation
- Checkout/payment screen
- Receipt/confirmation

Any misalignment signals compliance risk or customer confusion risk.

### Model Focus
Models A and B (transparency is the core differentiator)

### Survey Alignment
- Section D: Constraint tolerance
- Section E4, F4: Customer expectation fit

### Why This Mock Matters
Pre-tender pricing and transparency are **hard constraints**. If merchants don't believe customers will understand or accept the pricing flow, the model fails.

## Mock 03 — Merchant Setup Summary: Model A

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether merchants can operate Model A given its real constraints:
- Debit cannot be surcharged (debit inconsistency)
- Credit-only fee offset
- Required disclosures

### Tension Surfaced
**Simplicity vs coverage**

Model A is simpler to explain ("credit cards cost more") but only covers credit transactions. Merchants must accept that debit and cash do not offset fees.

### Model Focus
Model A (Credit Fee Model)

### Survey Alignment
- Section E: Model A scenario
- Section L: Staff operability
- Section L5: Minimum worthwhile coverage

### Why This Mock Matters
If merchants don't understand that debit is NOT covered, they'll have false expectations. This mock makes the limitation explicit.

---

## Mock 04 — Merchant Setup Summary: Model B

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether merchants trust system-calculated pricing and accept higher posted prices.

### Tension Surfaced
**Control vs convenience**

Model B offers better coverage (all card types priced to recover fees) but requires trusting the system to calculate the posted price. Higher visible prices may hurt competitiveness.

### Model Focus
Model B (Embedded Price + Discount)

### Survey Alignment
- Section F: Model B scenario
- Section J: Trust in calculation
- Section L: Retention confidence

### Why This Mock Matters
Model B is economically superior for coverage but psychologically harder. Merchants must accept:
1. Higher posted prices
2. System-calculated markup
3. Trusting that "discount" messaging works

## Mock 05 — Channel Matrix (Model C)

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether channel-based pricing is real or just theoretical.

Do merchants naturally think:
- "In-person is different from online"
- "I want different pricing by surface"

Or do they want:
- "One model everywhere"

### Tension Surfaced
**Consistency vs context-optimization**

Channel-based pricing may be operationally optimal (convenience fee online, surcharge in-person) but creates:
- Staff confusion
- Customer confusion
- Compliance complexity

### Model Focus
Model C (channel-based composition)

### Survey Alignment
- Section G: Channel-based thinking
- Section H: Model comparison

### Why This Mock Matters
If merchants don't naturally think in channel-based terms, forcing Model C creates unnecessary complexity. If they DO think this way, single-model-everywhere may feel rigid.

---

## Mock 06 — Manual Entry Edge Case

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether merchants understand how pricing models work outside of catalog/register flows.

### Tension Surfaced
**Structured vs unstructured workflows**

Manual entry exposes:
- What does merchant enter? (base or final?)
- What does system calculate?
- What does customer see before paying?

If the model doesn't work in manual entry, it may not work for invoice-heavy or services-heavy merchants.

### Model Focus
Models A and B (manual entry behavior differs significantly)

### Survey Alignment
- Section K: Manual entry
- Section L: Staff operability

### Why This Mock Matters
Many SMBs use manual card entry frequently. If the pricing model breaks in manual entry, it's a segment killer.

---

## Mock 07 — Coverage and Economics Summary

### Strategic Purpose
Ground merchant expectations in realistic economics.

Do NOT overclaim "100% zero fees" if:
- Debit mix is high (Model A)
- Cash mix is high (Model B discount reduces margin)
- Convenience fee is lower than surcharge caps

### Tension Surfaced
**Headline promise vs economic reality**

Merchants attracted by "0% fees" may expect 100% coverage. This mock shows:
- Actual offset % by model
- Remaining merchant cost
- Impact of payment mix

### Model Focus
All three models (comparative economics)

### Survey Alignment
- Section L5: Minimum worthwhile coverage
- Section M: Monetization
- Section H5: "Zero fees" threshold

### Why This Mock Matters
If merchants feel misled by the headline, trust collapses. Better to surface the coverage reality early.

---

## Mock 08 — Explanation Helper

### Strategic Purpose
Test whether staff can explain the model to customers without confusion or pushback.

### Tension Surfaced
**Merchant understanding vs customer understanding**

Even if a merchant intellectually understands the model, can their staff explain it consistently?

This mock provides:
- What to say
- What customers will ask
- What must be disclosed

### Model Focus
All three models

### Survey Alignment
- Section I: Language and terminology
- Section L: Staff operability
- Section E6, F7: Customer expectation fit

### Why This Mock Matters
If staff can't explain it, the model won't work in practice. This is an activation AND retention gating factor.

## What Success Looks Like

### For Model A
- Merchants accept debit inconsistency explicitly
- Merchants believe customers will tolerate visible fee
- Merchants trust required disclosures won't hurt brand

### For Model B
- Merchants trust system-calculated pricing
- Merchants accept higher posted prices
- Merchants believe "cash discount" messaging will work

### For Model C
- Merchants naturally think in channel-based terms
- Merchants don't see channel-based as "too complex"
- Merchants can articulate why different channels warrant different models

---

## What Failure Looks Like

### For Model A
- Merchants reject debit inconsistency as "not really zero fees"
- Merchants fear customer pushback on visible fee
- Merchants don't trust they can comply with disclosure requirements

### For Model B
- Merchants distrust system-calculated pricing
- Merchants reject higher posted prices as uncompetitive
- Merchants fear discount messaging will confuse customers

### For Model C
- Merchants want "one model everywhere"
- Merchants see channel-based as operational burden
- Merchants can't articulate when or why to use different models

Combination of messaging + pricing model drives switching

### H3
Model A vs Model B represent fundamentally different tradeoffs
→ **Mocks 01, 02, 03, 04 test this**

### H4
Some merchants prefer one model everywhere; others prefer channel-based
→ **Mock 05 tests this directly**

### H6
Trust in system-calculated markup is key gating factor for Model B
→ **Mock 04 tests this directly**

### H7
Debit and cash mix materially influence "zero fee" perception
→ **Mock 07 tests this directly**

## Research Iteration Plan

After first round of testing:

**If Model A wins but merchants fear customer pushback:**
→ Create mock showing customer education materials

**If Model B wins but merchants distrust calculation:**
→ Create mock showing margin/pricing control interface

**If Model C is confusing:**
→ Simplify to "most merchants use X everywhere, but you can customize by channel"

**If coverage concerns dominate:**
→ Create mock showing breakeven analysis by payment mix

**If staff operability is the blocker:**
→ Create mock showing training materials or POS prompts
