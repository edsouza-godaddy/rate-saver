Merchant Setup Summary

Model A — Card Fee Model (Surcharge)
Selected Model: Card Fee (Surcharge)
Plain Language: Customers see lower base prices. Credit card payments include a fee. Debit and cash do not.

Required Disclosures
Compliance Requirements
  • Entry signage: "Credit card payments include a 3% fee" (or similar clear language)
  • Menu/shelf signage: Fee disclosure must be visible where prices are shown
  • Checkout disclosure: Fee amount must be shown before payment method selection
  • Receipt: Fee must appear as separate line item
  • Debit restriction: Fee CANNOT be applied to debit cards (illegal)
State Restrictions: Surcharging is prohibited or restricted in some states. We'll help you confirm what's allowed in your location.

Key Limitations
What This Model Does NOT Do
  • Does not offset debit fees: Debit cannot be surcharged, so those transactions remain a merchant cost
  • Does not cover cash costs: Cash has no fee to offset
  • Customer pushback risk: Some customers may object to visible fees
  • Signage required: Disclosure requirements must be met
Best Fit For
  • Merchants with high credit card volume (>60%)
  • Businesses comfortable with visible pricing differences
  • Merchants who prefer lower posted prices
  • Industries where fees are commonly accepted (parking, utilities, etc.)
Staff Explanation Script
What Your Staff Should Say

Standard explanation:

"Our menu prices are the base price. If you pay with a credit card, there's a 3% card fee to cover processing costs. Debit and cash are the menu price with no extra fee."

If customer asks "why?":

"Credit card companies charge us fees to process payments. We keep our menu prices lower by passing that cost to customers who choose credit. Debit and cash don't have those fees."

If customer objects:

"I understand. You're welcome to pay with debit or cash at the menu price with no extra charge."
