Manual Card Entry Workflow

How pricing models work outside catalog/register flows
Why This Matters: Many merchants manually enter amounts without using a catalog or register. This workflow must respect pre-tender pricing constraints.
Model A — Card Fee Model (Manual Entry)
1
Merchant Action: Enter Base Amount
Merchant enters the service/item base price into the payment terminal or system.
Enter amount:
2
System Action: Show Payment Options
System displays pricing for each payment type before customer selects.
Payment Options:

Credit Card: $103.00 (includes 3% card fee)
Debit Card: $100.00
Cash: $100.00
3
Customer Action: Select Payment Method
Customer sees the pricing options and chooses how to pay. Final price is known before payment.
Customer selects: Credit Card
Amount to charge: $103.00
Constraint Compliance: Pricing is defined before payment method. System does not change price after card is presented. Compliant
Model B — Prices Include Fees (Manual Entry)
1
Merchant Action: Enter Fee-Inclusive Amount
Merchant enters the fee-inclusive price (or system calculates it from base cost).
Base cost: $100.00
Fee-inclusive price:
System may calculate fee-inclusive price automatically if configured
2
System Action: Show Payment Options with Discount
System displays the standard price and discount options before customer selects.
Payment Options:

Credit Card: $103.00
Debit Card: $103.00
Cash: $100.00 Save $3
ACH: $100.00 Save $3
3
Customer Action: Select Payment Method
Customer sees standard price and discount offers, chooses how to pay.
Customer selects: Cash
Amount to charge: $100.00 (discount applied)
Constraint Compliance: Fee-inclusive price is defined before payment method. Discount is offered, not a post-selection change. Compliant
Key Differences in Manual Entry
Aspect Model A Model B
What Merchant Enters Base amount ($100) Fee-inclusive amount ($103) or base with auto-calculation
System Calculation Adds fee to credit ($100 → $103) Subtracts discount for cash/ACH ($103 → $100)
Staff Simplicity Enter what customer was quoted Enter fee-inclusive price (may require training)
Customer Expectation Customer quoted $100, pays $103 (if credit) Customer quoted $103, may pay less ($100 if cash)
Risk Staff must communicate fee upfront Staff must understand fee-inclusive pricing
Edge Case: Phone Order with Manual Entry
Model A — Phone Order Flow

Customer: "How much for the service?"

Staff: "The base price is $100. If you pay by credit card, there's a 3% card fee, so it's $103. Debit and cash are $100."

Customer: "I'll pay by credit card."

Staff: [Enters $100, system shows $103 for credit card]

Final charge: $103

Staff must quote both base and card-fee price upfront
Model B — Phone Order Flow

Customer: "How much for the service?"

Staff: "The price is $103. If you pay by cash or ACH, you'll save $3, so it's $100."

Customer: "I'll pay by credit card."

Staff: [Enters $103, customer pays full price]

Final charge: $103

Staff quotes fee-inclusive price first, then mentions discount
What Would NOT Work (Violations)
❌ Non-Compliant Manual Entry Scenarios
Scenario 1: Post-Card Type Adjustment
Staff enters $100, customer swipes card, system detects "credit" and automatically changes to $103.
❌ Violates pre-tender pricing constraint
Scenario 2: Hidden Fee
Customer is quoted "$100 over the phone", staff enters $103 without explaining, customer is surprised by final charge.
❌ Violates transparency constraint
Scenario 3: Debit Surcharge
System adds 3% fee to debit card transactions in manual entry.
❌ Illegal — debit cannot be surcharged
Staff Training for Manual Entry
Model A — Staff Guidelines
  • Always quote both base price and card-fee price when asked
  • Enter the base amount into the system
  • Let the system display payment options to customer
  • Confirm final amount before processing
Model B — Staff Guidelines
  • Always quote the fee-inclusive (posted) price first
  • Mention discount opportunity for cash/ACH
  • Enter the fee-inclusive amount (or let system calculate)
  • Let the system apply discount if customer chooses cash/ACH
