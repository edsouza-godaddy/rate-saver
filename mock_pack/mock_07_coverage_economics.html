What Would It Take to Hit "True Zero"?
Model A — True Zero Scenario

Required payment mix:

  • 100% credit cards
  • 0% debit or cash

Reality: This is unrealistic for most merchants. Customers use debit frequently.

Model A cannot achieve "true zero" fees for most businesses due to debit inconsistency.
Model B — True Zero Scenario

Required payment mix:

  • 100% credit + debit cards
  • 0% cash

Reality: More achievable. Many businesses are 90%+ card payments. Cash discount cost is small.

Model B can get close to "true zero" if cash mix is low (e.g., online-heavy businesses).
Minimum Worthwhile Coverage
Is Partial Coverage Worth It?

Not every business needs 100% fee offset to benefit. Consider:

Model A (84% coverage):
  • $585/mo savings ($7,020/year)
  • Lower posted prices (competitive advantage)
  • Simpler to explain
Model B (90% coverage):
  • $538/mo net savings ($6,456/year)
  • No surprise fees (better CX)
  • Covers more transaction types
Key Question: Would you switch providers for $500-600/month in savings, even if it's not 100% of fees?
