Channel-Based Approach: Different sales channels may benefit from different pricing models. This lets you optimize for each context.

Why Channel-Based Pricing?
Potential Benefits
  • Context optimization: In-person and online have different customer expectations
  • Convenience fee flexibility: Easier to use online/invoice where it's more accepted
  • Better coverage mix: Use Model A in-person (lower prices), convenience fee online (better debit coverage)
  • Strategic control: You decide what works best for each context
Potential Challenges
  • Staff confusion: Different rules by channel may be hard to remember
  • Customer confusion: "Why is pricing different online vs in-person?"
  • Operational complexity: More moving parts to manage
  • Consistency concerns: Customers may perceive inconsistency as unfair

Alternative: Single Model Everywhere
If You Prefer Consistency
You don't have to use channel-based pricing. You can choose one model and apply it everywhere:
Model A Everywhere
Same card fee model across POS, invoice, and online. Simpler to explain, but credit-only coverage.
Model B Everywhere
Same fee-inclusive pricing across all channels. Higher posted prices everywhere, but better coverage.
Most common choice: Many merchants prefer one model everywhere for simplicity. Channel-based is for merchants who see meaningful differences between in-person and online customer expectations.
Staff Training Considerations
What Your Team Needs to Know
If Customer... Staff Should Say...
Pays in-person with credit "Credit card payments include a 3% card fee, so it's $103. Debit and cash are $100."
Asks why invoice is different "For invoices and online payments, we use a convenience fee structure. It works a bit differently, but the goal is the same — keeping costs low."
Complains about inconsistency "We use the pricing approach that works best for each way you pay. In-person and online have different costs and customer expectations."
Training is critical: Staff confusion is the biggest risk with channel-based pricing. If your team can't explain it clearly, consider using one model everywhere.
