Required Disclosures
Compliance Requirements
  • Posted price consistency: The price on your menu/shelf must match the standard price customers pay
  • Discount disclosure: "Pay less with cash/ACH" or similar language must be visible at point of sale
  • Checkout disclosure: Discount must be shown before payment method selection
  • Receipt: Should show list price, discount applied, and final total
Compliance Status: This model is compliant in all states when configured correctly. The posted price is the standard price — the discount is an optional benefit.

Key Advantage: Both credit AND debit transactions are covered by fee-inclusive pricing. Only cash/ACH discounts reduce margin slightly.
Key Tradeoffs
Challenges
  • Higher visible prices: Your posted prices will be ~3% higher than competitors who absorb fees
  • Trust in calculation: Requires trusting the system to calculate the right posted price
  • Customer perception: Some customers may not understand the discount framing
  • Competitive positioning: May be harder to compete on posted price
Benefits
  • No surprise fees: Customers never see an unexpected charge at checkout
  • Better coverage: Credit + debit are both covered (90%+ of transactions)
  • Positive framing: "Save with cash" feels better than "fee for cards"
  • Simpler compliance: Posted price consistency is straightforward
Staff Explanation Script
What Your Staff Should Say

Standard explanation:

"Our posted prices include all costs. If you pay with cash or ACH, you get a discount — that's $100 instead of $103."

If customer asks "why is it higher?":

"Our prices are set to cover all our costs, including card processing fees. But if you pay with cash or bank transfer, you'll save a few dollars."

If customer compares to competitor:

"We price transparently — all costs are included. Many businesses factor fees into their pricing too, but they don't offer a discount for cash like we do."
