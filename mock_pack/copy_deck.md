# Copy Deck — Language and Messaging Reference

This document provides the standard language and messaging for each pricing model. Use this for consistency across mocks, survey instruments, and product materials.

---

## Model A — Credit Fee Model

### Plain-Language Label
**"Card Fee Model"**

### Industry-Term Label
**"Surcharge"** (or "Credit Card Surcharge")

### One-Sentence Explanation
Customers see a lower base price, but credit card payments include an additional fee.

### Staff Explanation
"Our menu prices are the base price. If you pay with a credit card, there's a 3% card fee. Debit and cash are the menu price with no extra fee."

### Customer Explanation
**What the customer sees:**
- Menu/shelf price: $100
- At payment: "Credit card payments: $103 (includes 3% card fee). Debit or cash: $100."

### Disclosure Reminder
**Required:**
- Signage at entry or point of sale: "Credit card payments include a 3% fee"
- Fee must be disclosed before payment method is selected
- Fee amount must not exceed actual card processing cost
- Debit cards cannot be surcharged

### Key Tradeoffs
✅ Lower visible prices  
✅ Simpler mental model ("credit costs more")  
❌ Debit transactions not covered  
❌ Customers may object to visible fee  
❌ Disclosure and signage requirements

## Model B — Prices Include Fees + Cash/ACH Discount

### Plain-Language Label
**"Prices Include Fees, Pay Less with Cash/ACH"**

### Industry-Term Label
**"Dual Pricing"** or **"Cash Discount"**

### One-Sentence Explanation
Prices are set to cover card processing fees, and customers who pay with cash or ACH receive a discount.

### Staff Explanation
"Our posted prices include all costs. If you pay with cash or ACH, you get a discount — that's $100 instead of $103."

### Customer Explanation
**What the customer sees:**
- Menu/shelf/site price: $103
- At payment: "Total: $103. Pay with cash or ACH and save $3 (final price: $100)."

### Disclosure Reminder
**Required:**
- Posted price must be the standard "list" price
- Discount must be clearly disclosed at point of sale
- Both the list price and discounted price should be visible before payment selection
- Posted price must match what customers see on shelf/menu/site

### Key Tradeoffs
✅ All card types covered (credit + debit)  
✅ No surprise fee at checkout  
✅ Positive framing ("save with cash")  
❌ Higher visible prices (may hurt competitiveness)  
❌ Requires trust in system-calculated pricing  
❌ Discount may confuse some customers

## Model C — Channel-Based Model

### Plain-Language Label
**"Different Pricing by Channel"**

### Industry-Term Label
**"Multi-Channel Pricing Strategy"**

### One-Sentence Explanation
Merchants choose the pricing model that works best for each sales channel (in-person, invoice, online).

### Staff Explanation
"We handle pricing differently depending on how you pay. In-person, we use [Model A or B]. For invoices and online, we use [Model A, B, or convenience fee]."

### Customer Explanation
**What the customer sees:**
Depends on the channel and selected model. Examples:

**In-person (Model A):**
- Menu price: $100
- Credit card: $103
- Debit/cash: $100

**Online (Convenience Fee):**
- Item price: $100
- Convenience fee for card payments: $3
- ACH: no fee

### Disclosure Reminder
**Required:**
- Each channel must independently comply with its selected model's disclosure requirements
- Customers must see clear pricing logic before payment in every channel
- Convenience fee is only applicable to invoice/online, not POS

### Key Tradeoffs
✅ Flexibility to optimize by channel  
✅ Can match customer expectations by context (online vs in-person)  
❌ More complex to explain and operate  
❌ Staff may confuse rules across channels  
❌ Customer may perceive inconsistency

## Convenience Fee (Online/Invoice Only)

### Plain-Language Label
**"Convenience Fee"**

### Industry-Term Label
**"Convenience Fee"** (standard term)

### One-Sentence Explanation
For online or invoice payments, a small fee is added for card payments; ACH is free.

### Staff Explanation (Invoice Context)
"There's a $3 convenience fee for paying by card. If you pay by bank transfer (ACH), there's no fee."

### Customer Explanation
**What the customer sees:**
- Invoice amount: $100
- Card payment: $103 (includes $3 convenience fee)
- ACH payment: $100 (no fee)

### Disclosure Reminder
**Required:**
- Fee must be disclosed before payment method selection
- Convenience fee applies to both credit AND debit cards (unlike surcharge)
- Only use for online/invoice, NOT in-person POS

### Key Tradeoffs
✅ Applies to both credit and debit  
✅ Well-understood term for online payments  
❌ Cannot be used in-person  
❌ May be lower than surcharge caps (less offset)

## Terminology Guidelines

### Prefer Plain Language
Use industry terms in parentheses for clarity, but lead with plain language:
- "Card fee (surcharge)" not just "surcharge"
- "Prices include fees, pay less with cash" not just "dual pricing"

### Avoid Jargon Overload
Do not use:
- "Blended rate optimization"
- "Dynamic pricing logic"
- "Cost-plus pass-through"

These terms confuse merchants and imply behavior that violates constraints.

### Be Explicit About Debit
Always clarify debit behavior:
- ✅ "Credit card payments include a fee. Debit and cash do not."
- ❌ "Card payments include a fee." (ambiguous — debit is a card)

### Frame Positively Where Possible
- ✅ "Pay less with cash" (positive frame)
- ❌ "Cash avoids the fee" (negative frame)

But do not hide downsides:
- ✅ "Higher posted prices, but no surprise fees"
- ❌ "No surprise fees" (hides the tradeoff)

## State/Jurisdiction Considerations

### Surcharge Restrictions
Some states prohibit or restrict surcharging. Do not claim "available everywhere" for Model A.

Standard disclaimer:
"Surcharging is subject to state and network rules. We'll help you confirm what's allowed in your state."

### Posted Price Laws
Some jurisdictions require that advertised price = charged price. Model B complies. Model A complies if fee is disclosed. 

Standard disclaimer:
"All models comply with price transparency laws when configured correctly. We'll guide you through required disclosures."

## "Zero Fees" Positioning

### When to Use
Use "0% credit card processing fees" in:
- Headline messaging
- Acquisition materials
- High-level positioning

### When to Clarify
Always clarify with:
- "How we offset fees depends on the pricing model you choose"
- "Coverage depends on your payment mix"

### Never Claim
- "100% of transactions are fee-free" (unless truly supported by model + payment mix)
- "No cost to you or your customers" (merchant or customer pays, depending on model)
- "Fees disappear" (fees are offset, not eliminated)

## Explanation Scripts by Merchant Segment

### Restaurant/Cafe (High Cash/Debit Mix)
**Model A:** "Your menu prices stay low. Credit card customers pay a small fee, but most of your debit and cash customers don't."

**Model B:** "We calculate menu prices that cover your costs. Cash customers save a few dollars, and you don't have to explain fees."

### Retail (Mixed Payment Types)
**Model A:** "Shelf prices stay competitive. Customers who choose credit pay a bit more."

**Model B:** "Your prices are set to keep your margins whole. Cash and ACH customers get a discount."

### Services/Invoice-Heavy (Flexible Context)
**Model A (invoice):** "Your invoice amount is the base price. Credit card payments include a surcharge. ACH is the invoice amount."

**Convenience Fee (invoice):** "Your invoice is $X. There's a convenience fee for card payments. ACH is free."

## Copy Testing Notes

When testing messaging:
- Watch for confusion between "card" (credit+debit) and "credit card" (credit only)
- Test both "card fee" and "surcharge" language
- Test both "cash discount" and "pay less with cash" language
- Measure which framing reduces objection rate
- Track which terminology merchants use naturally when explaining back

Language that merchants adopt and repeat is language that works.
