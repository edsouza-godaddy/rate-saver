Staff Explanation Helper

What to say, what customers will ask, what must be disclosed
Purpose: This guide helps your staff explain pricing consistently and handle customer questions confidently.
Model A — Card Fee Model (Surcharge)
Plain Language: "Card Fee"
Industry Term: Surcharge
Primary Explanation (What to Say):

"Our menu prices are the base price. If you pay with a credit card, there's a 3% card fee to cover processing costs. Debit and cash are the menu price with no extra fee."
Common Customer Questions & Responses:
Customer Asks Staff Should Say
"Why is there a fee?" "Credit card companies charge us fees to process payments. We keep our menu prices lower by passing that cost to customers who choose credit."
"Can I avoid the fee?" "Yes! If you pay with debit or cash, it's the menu price with no extra charge."
"Is this legal?" "Yes, it's allowed in most states. The fee only applies to credit cards — debit and cash have no fee."
"Why didn't you tell me?" "I apologize if you missed it. There's signage at the entrance and on the menu showing that credit card payments include a fee. The amount is shown before you pay."
"Other places don't charge this." "Some businesses absorb the cost, which often means higher prices overall. We prefer to keep our base prices low and let customers choose."
"What about debit?" "Debit cards are the menu price — no fee. Only credit cards have the processing fee."
Required Disclosures:
  • Entry signage: "Credit card payments include a 3% fee"
  • Menu/price signage must mention fee
  • Fee shown at checkout before payment
  • Receipt shows fee as separate line item
Never Say: "All cards have a fee" (debit does NOT). Always clarify "credit card" specifically.
Model B — Prices Include Fees + Cash/ACH Discount
Plain Language: "Prices Include Fees, Pay Less with Cash/ACH"
Industry Term: Cash Discount / Dual Pricing
Primary Explanation (What to Say):

"Our posted prices include all costs. If you pay with cash or bank transfer, you get a discount — that's $100 instead of $103."
Common Customer Questions & Responses:
Customer Asks Staff Should Say
"Why is it more expensive than [competitor]?" "Our prices include all costs transparently. Many businesses factor fees into pricing too. We offer a discount if you pay with cash or bank transfer — you can save a few dollars."
"How much is the discount?" "Cash and bank transfer customers save 3% — for a $103 item, that's $100 with the discount."
"What if I pay with debit?" "Debit cards pay the posted price, same as credit. The discount is for cash and bank transfers."
"Why don't you just lower the price?" "Card processing has real costs. We price to cover those costs. Customers who pay with cash or ACH save money because those methods cost less to process."
"Is this a credit card fee?" "It's not an extra fee — this is our standard price. We offer a discount for cash and bank transfers to pass savings along to those customers."
"Can I use a credit card?" "Absolutely. Credit and debit are both the posted price. Cash and bank transfer get the discount."
Required Disclosures:
  • Posted price must match standard/list price
  • Signage: "Pay less with cash/ACH" or similar
  • Discount shown at checkout before payment
  • Receipt shows discount as line item
Never Say: "There's a card fee, but we call it a discount." Be clear: posted price IS the standard price; discount is a benefit.
Model C — Channel-Based Pricing
Plain Language: "Different Pricing by Channel"
Primary Explanation (What to Say):

"We handle pricing differently depending on how you pay. In-person, [explain POS model]. For invoices and online, [explain invoice/online model]."
Example Combinations:
In-Person (Model A)
"Our menu prices are the base price. Credit cards pay a 3% card fee. Debit and cash are the menu price."
Invoice/Online (Convenience Fee)
"The invoice amount is $100. Card payments include a $3 convenience fee. Bank transfer (ACH) has no fee."
Common Customer Questions & Responses:
Customer Asks Staff Should Say
"Why is it different online vs in-person?" "Different payment contexts work better with different pricing approaches. In-person and online have different costs and customer expectations. The goal is the same — keeping costs transparent."
"That's confusing." "I understand. The key is: you'll always see the final price before you pay, whether you're here in person or paying online."
"Which way should I pay?" "Debit and cash have no extra fees in-person. For invoices, bank transfer (ACH) has no fee. Credit cards work everywhere but may include a processing fee."
High Risk: Channel-based pricing is hardest to explain. Staff must clearly understand which model applies to each channel. Consider extra training.
Language Comparison
Concept Plain Language (Recommended) Industry Term
Fee for credit cards "Card fee" "Surcharge"
Lower price for non-card "Pay less with cash" "Cash discount"
Online/invoice fee "Convenience fee" "Convenience fee" (standard)
Fee-inclusive pricing "Prices include fees" "Dual pricing"
Recommendation: Use plain language in customer conversations. Staff can learn industry terms for internal discussions, but customers understand plain language better.
Training Checklist for Staff
✓ Staff Must Know
  • Which pricing model you use (A, B, or C)
  • What customers see at each step (shelf → checkout → receipt)
  • How to explain the model in one sentence
  • Which payment types have fees/discounts
  • Where disclosure signage is located
  • How to handle objections calmly
✓ Staff Must NOT
  • Say "all cards have a fee" (clarify credit only for Model A)
  • Hide or downplay the fee/pricing structure
  • Make up answers if unsure (ask manager)
  • Argue with customers who object
  • Apply pricing inconsistently
  • Confuse channel-based rules (if using Model C)
Role-Play Scenarios
Practice these with your team
Scenario 1: First-Time Customer (Model A)

Customer: "How much is this?"

Staff: "The menu price is $100. If you pay with a credit card, there's a 3% card fee, so it's $103. Debit and cash are $100."

Customer: "Oh, I didn't know about the fee."

Staff: "No problem! It's posted on our signage. You can avoid the fee by using debit or cash — those are the menu price."

Scenario 2: Price-Conscious Customer (Model B)

Customer: "This is more expensive than [competitor]."

Staff: "Our pricing is transparent — all costs are included. If you pay with cash or bank transfer, you'll get a discount. That brings it to $100, which is competitive."

Customer: "Okay, I'll pay with cash then."

Staff: "Great! That's $100 with the cash discount."

Scenario 3: Objecting Customer (Model A)

Customer: "I'm not paying a fee. This is ridiculous."

Staff: "I understand your frustration. You're welcome to pay with debit or cash at the menu price with no fee. Would one of those work for you?"

Customer: "Fine. I'll use debit."

Staff: "Perfect. That's $100 — no fee."

Staff Confidence Assessment
Can your team explain this model consistently?
Test Questions for Staff:
  1. Can you explain our pricing model in one sentence?
  2. What does a customer pay if they use a credit card? Debit? Cash?
  3. Where is the fee/discount disclosed to customers?
  4. A customer asks "why is there a fee?" What do you say?
  5. A customer objects and refuses to pay the fee. What do you do?
If staff cannot answer these confidently: The model may be too complex, or more training is needed before launch.
