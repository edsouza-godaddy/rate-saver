Product Requirements Document

1. CONTEXT & STRATEGIC POSITIONING

This initiative delivers a cash + ACH discounting platform capability that enables merchants to achieve true "zero fees" by embedding processing costs into list prices and offering discounts for preferred payment methods.

Strategic Evolution
This capability represents the evolution of our fee-passing offerings from surcharge-only (credit cards) to comprehensive tender-type-driven discounting (cash, ACH, and future tender types). Current Rate Saver offers surcharge on credit cards, leaving debit processing fees with the merchant and blocking/capping merchants in surcharge-restricted states. This capability fills that gap.

Positioning Flexibility
The underlying capability is designed to support multiple go-to-market strategies:
•	Rate Saver enhancement: Add cash discounting mode alongside existing surcharge
•	Standalone feature: Offer directly to merchants as a free or paid feature
•	Direct to resellers: Offer to resellers unbundled from Rate Saver (e.g. how surcharge is offered today)
•	Bundled offering: Package with other commerce features

This PRD describes the capability. GTM positioning decisions (Rate Saver vs standalone vs bundled) are business decisions that can be made independently of the technical implementation.

Building Flexibility for Future
The platform must be architected to support future extensions:
•	ACH can be decoupled from cash: e.g. different discount rates, or ACH excluded from discount entirely.
•	Additional tender types: Gift cards, BNPL, digital wallets may have each have their own discount rates.
•	Ability to select different approaches per channel - e.g. Surcharge in POS, ACH discount in Invoicing.
•	Ability to support different presentation modes on surfaces - e.g. List Price only, List Price with Discount, List Price and Discounted Price. 

MVP focuses on cash + ACH bundled together with a single discount rate, but the underlying system should anticipate per-tender-type discount flexibility, with additional channel dimensionality. 

Relationship to Existing Rate Saver:
•	Mutually exclusivity: Merchants choose surcharge OR cash discounting, not both simultaneously
•	Future state: Channel-based pricing where, for e.g. surcharge applies to POS and cash discount applies to invoicing (only an example)
•	Existing Rate Saver merchants: Surcharge merchants remain on surcharge unless they actively choose to switch to cash discounting.


Primary Customer
Direct GoDaddy Payments merchants are the primary customer. Reseller merchants (including Dough and others) inherit the same merchant experience with distributor-level configuration controls.


Why Cash Discounting?
Cash discounting is being pursued because it uniquely provides:
•	True zero processing fees for merchants, including debit
•	Applicability across all 50 states (surcharge is restricted or capped in several states)
•	Coverage across all payment methods (credit, debit, ACH, cash)
•	Fewer network constraints and reduced long-term regulatory risk
•	A simpler merchant and sales narrative: fees are built into the list price

2. LEGAL & COMPLIANCE CONSTRAINTS

The following constraints impact product & UX and must be preserved across all surfaces.

Discounts Must Never Be Linked to Processing Costs
If the discount is framed or interpreted as "covering processing fees," it will be treated as a surcharge, and surcharging debit is illegal. Therefore, discount logic must be decoupled from processing fees in all customer-facing language.

Downward Discount Only
Customers pay a lower price (receive a discount) for preferred methods (cash/ACH). Fees are never "added" at checkout, that's an illegal surcharge.

All States, No Caps
Unlike surcharging, cash discounting / dual pricing:
•	Is legal in all 50 U.S. states
•	Has no cap on the price difference
•	Applies across all card types (credit and debit)

Mutual Exclusivity with Surcharge
Merchants cannot enable Rate Saver surcharge and cash discounting simultaneously for MVP. Future state may allow channel-based usage (surcharge on POS, ACH discount on invoicing).

Pricing Visibility is Mandatory
Wherever a consumer sees a price, the experience must match the chosen model:
•	Cash discount → Higher prices from the first exposure, discount disclosed at cart, checkout and on receipt

Violating any of the above creates legal and compliance risk for both merchants and GoDaddy.

3. PRODUCT MECHANICS

Cash Discounting vs Dual Pricing
Although often used interchangeably, these are two different presentations of the same underlying capability.

Cash Discounting (MVP - Single Price + Discount Line)
•	Listed price per item is the card (higher) price
•	A separate, explicit discount line item is shown when the customer pays with cash or ACH
•	The discount must appear at checkout and on the receipt
•	Consumers initially see a single price, with the discount applied at payment selection

Example:
•	Item 1: $105
•	Item 2: $210
•	Subtotal: $315
•	Cash / ACH discount (3%): –$9.45
•	Net paid (cash/ACH): $305.55

Dual Pricing (Future - Not MVP)
•	Two prices are always displayed on all consumer-facing surfaces (shelf, menu, order line items, checkout)
•	Every item has a list price and a cash/ACH price
•	At checkout and on receipts: no separate discount line item required; only the selected price is shown

Example:
•	Item 1: Card $105 / Cash $101.85
•	Item 2: Card $210 / Cash $203.70
•	Card Subtotal: $315 / Cash Subtotal: $305.55

MVP focuses on cash discounting (single price + discount line). Dual pricing might be a future enhancement, which should be only a presentation change, with the same underlying cash discounting capabilities. 

Core Calculation Engine

Merchant Configuration Inputs:
•	Discount percentage (e.g., 3%)
•	Auto-calculate list prices: ON or OFF
•	Rounding rule - Applicable only if Auto-Calculate is ON: (Round up to nearest $0.01, $0.05, $0.25, $0.50, $1.00)


Auto-Calculate List Prices = ON (Recommended Default)
Merchant enters their desired cash/ACH price (what they want to net). System calculates the list price by backing out the discount with rounding.

Formula:
  Cash Price = $100 (merchant's target)
  List Price = $100 ÷ (1 - 0.03) = $103.09 → round to $103.25 (if rounding to nearest $0.25)
  At checkout: Card customers pay $103.25, cash customers get 3% discount = $100.15

This mode ensures merchants achieve their target net revenue and processing costs are offset.

Auto-Calculate List Prices = OFF (Pure Cash Discounting)
Merchant enters the list price. Cash/ACH customers receive a discount off that price at checkout.

Formula:
  List Price = $100 (merchant sets)
  Cash/ACH Discount = $100 × 3% = $3.00
  Cash/ACH Price = $100 - $3.00 = $97.00

This mode is traditional cash discounting. Merchants using this mode do NOT fully offset processing fees—they give up margin to incentivize cash.

Why Offer Both?
•	Some merchants prefer auto-calculate (offset processing fees, achieve target revenue)
•	Some merchants prefer pure cash discounting (transparent list price, give discount as incentive)
•	Offering both as a merchant control allows flexibility without picking a single approach
•	If future product decisions require forcing one mode, it's easier to default all merchants and hide the toggle.

Tax Treatment
Tax is calculated on the list price for all payment methods in cash discounting mode (MVP).

Example:
  List Price: $103.25
  Tax (10%): $10.33 (calculated on $103.25)

  Card payment: $103.25 + $10.33 = $113.58
  Cash payment: $103.25 - $3.10 discount = $100.15, + $10.33 tax = $110.48

This ensures tax basis is consistent and compliant.
Discounts Apply to Subtotal Only
Discounts are applied to the item/order subtotal, not to tax. Tax is calculated separately and added after discount.

Note: Assumption that needs to be validated with Tax experts.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

5.1 Core Calculation Engine

System must support:
•	Standard Price (list price)
•	Preferred Method Price (cash/ACH discounted price)

Calculation Modes:
a) Auto-Calculate ON:
   •	Input: Cash/ACH price (merchant's target net revenue)
   •	Output: List price = cash price ÷ (1 - discount%) with rounding applied
   •	Discount amount = list price × discount%
   •	Cash/ACH price = list price - discount

b) Auto-Calculate OFF:
   •	List price = merchant's advertised price
   •	Discount amount = list price × discount%
   •	Cash/ACH price = list price - discount

Rounding Rules:
System must support rounding to nearest:
•	$0.00 (no rounding)
•	$0.05 
•	$0.25 ← recommended default
•	$0.50
•	$0.99 
•	$1.00

Tax Calculation:
•	Tax calculated on list price for all payment methods (MVP)
•	Discount applies to subtotal only, not tax basis

Discount Application:
•	Downward only (never add fees at checkout)
•	Applied at payment method selection or order finalization
•	Shown as separate line item on receipt

No Upward Price Add-On:
System must prevent any upward price adjustment at checkout. Fees are embedded in list price; discounts reduce from that price. 
Fee/Markup terminology must never appear in customer-facing or merchant-facing UIs or reports.

5.2 Merchant Configuration & Onboarding

Merchant Eligibility:
•	All U.S. merchants are eligible (cash discounting is legal in all 50 states)
•	Merchant must NOT have surcharge enabled (mutual exclusivity)
•	Merchant must have ACH enabled for invoicing/paylinks (if those surfaces support ACH discount)

Configuration UI (Commerce Home):
Merchant must be able to:
a) Enable/Disable Cash Discounting
b) Set Discount Percentage
c) Auto-Calculate List Prices Toggle
d) Choose Rounding Rule
e) Preview / Calculator
   •	Live preview showing example:
     - "If you enter $100 (cash price), customers will see $103.25 (list price) with your settings."
   •	Or if auto-calculate OFF:
     - "If you set list price at $100, cash/ACH customers pay $97.00 after discount."

Attestation:
Merchant must attest before enabling cash discounting:
(i) "I understand pricing visibility obligations: All merchandising materials (menus, boards, brochures, website) will reflect the pricing model (list price with discount for cash/ACH)."
(ii) "I will place visible signage stating my cash discount policy (e.g., 'Save 3% when you pay with cash or ACH')."
(iii) "I am independently setting prices/discounts and not linking them to processing fees."
(iv) Attestation timestamp and content must be logged.

Audit Logging:
System must log:
•	Feature activation timestamp
•	Attestation acceptance and content
•	All configuration changes (discount %, rounding, auto-calculate toggle)
•	Timestamp and user ID for each change

================================================================================
5.8 POST-TRANSACTION MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

Refunds:
•	Full Refund:
   - Refund the amount customer paid (list price or cash/ACH discounted price)
   - Reverse discount proportionally if cash/ACH payment

•	Partial Refund:
   - Refund must be issued proportionally based on original payment method
   - If original payment was cash/ACH with discount, partial refund maintains discount proportion
   - Example: $100 item, $97 paid after 3% cash discount. $50 partial refund = $48.50 (maintains 3% discount proportion)

Voids:
•	Void must reverse the exact authorized amount (list price or discounted price)

Chargebacks:
•	Chargeback amount reflects what customer was actually charged (list price or discounted price)
•	Merchant liability is based on charged amount, not list price

Order & Transaction Dashboards:
a) Transaction View:
   •	Display payment method (card, cash, ACH)
   •	Display amount charged (list price or discounted price)
   •	Display discount amount (if applicable)
   •	Subtotal, discount line, tax, total must reconcile

b) Order Reports:
   •	Filter by payment method
   •	Show discount amounts as separate column
   •	Aggregate discount totals for reporting period

c) Order Item Reports:
   •	Show item-level pricing (list price)
   •	Show payment method and discount applied (order-level, not item-level)
   •	Ensure prices reconcile across all views

5.9 Additional Use Cases

Tips / Gratuity:
•	Tip calculated on final amount (post-discount for cash/ACH, list price for card)
•	Tip is NOT subject to cash discount (discount applies to goods/services subtotal only)
•	Receipt must show: Subtotal, Discount (if applicable), Tip (separate line), Tax, Total

Taxes:
•	Tax calculated on subtotal (list price) for all payment methods
•	Discount applies to subtotal only, not tax basis
•	Tax shown separately on receipt

Promotions / Coupons:
•	If merchant has other promotional discounts (coupons, promos), order of operations:
   1. Promotional discount applied first (e.g., 10% off coupon)
   2. Subtotal after promo discount
   3. Tax calculated on post-promo subtotal
   4. Cash/ACH discount applied to subtotal (not to tax)
   5. Total

•	Stacking order must be documented and consistent
•	Merchant can configure stacking behavior (allow/disallow multiple discounts)

Split Tender:
•	If customer pays with multiple methods (e.g., $50 cash + $50 card), discount applies only to cash portion
•	Example: $100 order, 3% cash discount
   - Customer pays $50 cash + $50 card
   - Cash portion: $50 - $1.50 discount = $48.50
   - Card portion: $50 (no discount)
   - Total paid: $48.50 + $50 = $98.50

•	System must calculate and apply discount proportionally to cash/ACH portion only
•	Receipt must show split tender breakdown

5.10 Audit Logging

System must log:
•	Feature activation/deactivation timestamp
•	Attestation acceptance (full text + timestamp + merchant ID)
•	Configuration changes:
   - Discount percentage changes (old value, new value, timestamp, user ID)
   - Rounding rule changes
   - Auto-calculate toggle changes
•	Payment method used per transaction
•	Discount amounts applied per transaction
•	Refund/void events with discount reversal details

Logs must be:
•	Immutable (append-only)
•	Queryable for compliance audits
•	Retained for minimum 7 years (compliance requirement)

6. RESELLER / DISTRIBUTOR MODEL

Cash Discounting capability must support both models with appropriate control distribution.

Distributor-Level Configuration
Reseller/distributor admins can configure the settings and overrides for their merchant base.

Distributor Admin Controls:
a) Enable/Disable Cash Discounting for Distributor
   •	Toggle: ON / OFF
   •	If OFF, cash discounting is hidden from all distributor merchants (they cannot see or enable it)
   •	If ON, distributor can configure defaults below

b) Default Discount Percentage
   •	Set distributor-wide default (e.g., 3%)
   •	Merchants inherit this value
   •	Distributor can choose: Allow merchant override (YES/NO)
     - If NO: Merchant cannot change discount %, sees it as read-only
     - If YES: Merchant can edit discount % within allowed range

c) Default Rounding Rule
   •	Set distributor-wide default (e.g., $0.25)
   •	Distributor can choose: Allow merchant override (YES/NO)

d) Auto-Calculate List Prices Default
   •	Set distributor-wide default (ON or OFF)
   •	Distributor can choose: Allow merchant override (YES/NO)
   •	If NO: All distributor merchants use the same mode (e.g., auto-calculate always ON), toggle is hidden from merchant UI

e) Fee Program Selection (Surcharge vs Cash Discount)
   •	Distributor can set default: Surcharge or Cash Discount
   •	Distributor can choose: Allow merchant override (YES/NO)
     - If NO: Merchant cannot switch between surcharge and cash discount; distributor controls this
     - If YES: Merchant can toggle between surcharge and cash discount (mutual exclusivity enforced)

Merchant Experience Under Distributor:
•	If distributor locks a setting: Merchant sees it as read-only or hidden
•	If distributor allows override: Merchant sees full configuration UI
•	Merchant is unaware whether a setting is distributor-controlled or self-controlled (seamless experience)
•	Attestation still required from merchant (even if distributor controls settings)

Example: Dough Configuration
Dough (reseller) wants:
•	All Dough merchants use cash discounting (not surcharge)
•	3.5% discount rate locked (merchants cannot change)
•	Auto-calculate list prices always ON (locked)
•	Rounding to nearest $0.25 (locked)

Dough admin sets:
•	Fee program: Cash Discount (locked)
•	Discount %: 3% (locked, no merchant override)
•	Auto-calculate: ON (locked, no merchant override)
•	Rounding: $0.25 (locked, no merchant override)

Dough merchants see:
•	Simplified UI showing their pricing model is active
•	Cannot access toggle or configuration fields (hidden or read-only)
•	Still complete attestation (required for compliance)

Implementation Notes:
•	Distributor-level config stored separately from merchant-level config
•	Merchant settings inherit from distributor defaults but can be overridden if allowed
•	API must distinguish between distributor-set and merchant-set values
•	UI must clearly indicate when a setting is locked by distributor (e.g., "Managed by your payment partner")

•	Accurate pricing across all surfaces: List price, cash/ACH price, discount amounts, tax calculations all reconcile correctly
•	Merchant activation: ≥8% of direct SaaS merchants enable cash discounting within 90 days of launch (20% of RateSaver)
•	Merchant retention: ≥20% of merchants who activate remain active after 6 months 
•	Transaction accuracy: 100% of transactions reconcile correctly (no discrepancies in discount application, tax calculation, or totals)

Failure Metrics (Trigger Re-Evaluation):
•	Compliance incident: Any legal escalation or network violation
•	Merchant confusion: ≥10% of activating merchants contact support due to pricing confusion
•	Pricing errors: ≥1% of transactions have incorrect discount or tax calculations
•	Operational issues: Critical bugs blocking transaction completion or receipt generation

Hard Requirements:
•	Zero compliance incidents (absolute requirement)
•	No surcharge misclassification (cash discount must never be treated as surcharge)
•	Accurate price presentation on all surfaces (invoices, mobile, receipts, POS, paylinks, portal)
•	Merchant attestation logged for 100% of activations

Long-Term Success (6-12 Months Post-Launch):
•	Direct merchant adoption: Cash discounting grows to 50% of Rate Saver signups.
•	GPV growth: Measurable GPV increase attributed to zero fees positioning
•	Margin uplift: GCR improvement from pricing power (merchants not absorbing fees → higher effective rates)
•	Reseller adoption: Multiple resellers (beyond Dough) adopt cash discounting for their merchant base
•	Product evolution: Successfully launch dual pricing mode, channel-based pricing, per-tender-type discount rates

8. OUT OF SCOPE

The following are explicitly out of scope for MVP and should not be included in Phase 1 delivery:

Migration of Existing Rate Saver Merchants:
•	No automatic migration from surcharge to cash discounting
•	No proactive outreach or migration campaigns
•	Existing surcharge merchants remain on surcharge unless they opt in to cash discounting
•	Migration strategy is a GTM/CS decision, not a product requirement

Dual Pricing Display Mode:
•	MVP uses single price + discount line only
•	Dual pricing (two prices shown on shelf/menu/invoice) is a future enhancement
•	System architecture should anticipate dual pricing, but UI/UX is out of scope for MVP

Channel-Based Pricing (Surcharge + Cash Discount Simultaneously):
•	MVP enforces mutual exclusivity (surcharge OR cash discount, not both)
•	Future state may allow surcharge on POS + cash discount on invoicing
•	Not required for MVP

Per-Tender-Type Discount Rates:
•	MVP bundles cash + ACH into a single discount rate
•	Future state allows: Cash 3%, ACH 2%, Gift Card 5%, etc.
•	System architecture should anticipate this, but configuration UI is out of scope for MVP

9. DEPENDENCIES & ASSUMPTIONS

Dependencies:
•	ACH payment support: Merchants must have ACH enabled for invoicing/paylinks to offer ACH discount (POS/mobile support cash only)
•	Merchant attestation flow: Legal/compliance must approve attestation text
•	Receipt generation: Existing receipt infrastructure must support additional line item (discount)
•	Tax calculation engine: Must support tax-on-subtotal logic (already exists, assuming no changes needed)

Assumptions:
•	Merchants understand basic fee-passing concepts (surcharge, cash discount)
•	Merchants can set/change prices in their catalogs if auto-calculate is ON (requires updating cash prices, system calculates list prices)
•	Existing Rate Saver infrastructure can be extended or paralleled (architecture decision left to engineering)
•	Resellers/distributors have admin access to configure merchant defaults

Risks:
•	Merchant confusion: If auto-calculate logic is not explained clearly, merchants may not understand which price to enter
   - Mitigation: Strong onboarding UX, tooltips, live preview calculator
•	Customer pushback: Customers may object to higher list prices or perceive cash discount as "penalty"
   - Mitigation: Merchant education on positioning, signage requirements, clear disclosure
•	Tax compliance: Tax calculation on subtotal (not discounted price) must be verified with tax advisors
   - Mitigation: Legal/compliance review before launch, clearly documented tax treatment
•	Reseller adoption: If resellers prefer surcharge, cash discounting may not gain traction in reseller channel
   - Mitigation: Business case and education for resellers on state restrictions and debit coverage benefits

10. GLOSSARY

Cash Discounting: A pricing model where the list price is the card price, and customers who pay with cash or ACH receive a discount. Legal in all 50 U.S. states.

Dual Pricing: A pricing model where two prices are displayed for the same item (card price and cash price). A presentation approach of cash discounting.

Surcharge: An additional fee added to the transaction when a customer pays with a credit card. Cannot be applied to debit cards. Subject to state restrictions and caps.

Auto-Calculate List Prices: A merchant setting where the merchant enters their desired cash/ACH price (net revenue target), and the system calculates the list price by backing out the discount with rounding.

Tender Type: The payment method used (cash, credit card, debit card, ACH, gift card, etc.).

Preferred Payment Method: The payment methods eligible for discount (cash, ACH). In MVP, cash and ACH are bundled with the same discount rate.

List Price: The advertised price (typically the card price in cash discounting mode).

Rounding Rule: The increment to which prices are rounded (e.g., nearest $0.25) for customer clarity.

Attestation: A legal acknowledgment by the merchant that they understand pricing obligations and are independently setting prices (not linking discount to processing fees).

Distributor: A reseller partner who onboards merchants under their brand/relationship (e.g., Dough, Moolah).

Mutual Exclusivity: Surcharge and cash discounting cannot be enabled simultaneously for the same merchant.
